Caesar Salad

The Caesar Salad is a timeless dish that has stood the test of time and remains one of the most popular salads worldwide. Originating in the 1920s, it was created by Italian-American restaurateur Caesar Cardini in Tijuana, Mexico. Legend has it that during a busy Fourth of July weekend, Cardini faced a shortage of ingredients in his kitchen. Using what was available, he combined romaine lettuce, croutons, Parmesan cheese, and a unique dressing, crafting a simple yet extraordinary salad. What was initially a spur-of-the-moment invention quickly gained fame, making its way onto menus across the globe. The defining feature of a Caesar Salad is its dressing, which is bold and packed with flavor. Traditionally made with olive oil, egg yolk, garlic, lemon juice, Worcestershire sauce, Dijon mustard, and anchovies, it delivers a tangy, savory punch. The anchovies, while optional in modern adaptations, are essential in the classic version, adding a layer of umami that sets the dressing apart. Freshly grated Parmesan cheese is whisked into the dressing, enhancing its creamy texture and providing a nutty, salty kick.

Ingredients

- 6 cloves peeled garlic
- 6 tablespoons grated Parmesan Cheese
- 5 minced anchovies
- 1 tablespoon Lemon Juice
- 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
- 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
- Salt and ground black pepper
- 1/4 cup olive oil
- 4 cups day-old bread
- 1 head romaine lettuce
