Potato Salad

Potato Salad is a beloved dish enjoyed around the world, often served as a side at barbecues, picnics, and family gatherings. Its origins are thought to trace back to Germany, where warm potato salads were dressed with vinegar and mustard. However, the creamy, mayonnaise-based version popular in the United States is now a classic comfort food staple. The salad typically features boiled, tender potatoes as the main ingredient, often combined with hard-boiled eggs, chopped celery, onions, and pickles for crunch and flavor. Some versions include fresh herbs like parsley or dill to enhance the freshness of the dish. The dressing is usually a creamy mixture of mayonnaise, mustard, vinegar, and seasonings, though variations with sour cream or yogurt are also common.
