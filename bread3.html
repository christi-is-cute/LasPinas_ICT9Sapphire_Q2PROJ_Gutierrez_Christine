Brioche

Brioche is a soft, buttery bread known for its rich, slightly sweet flavor and airy texture. It is made with a high proportion of butter, eggs, milk, and sugar, which gives it its distinct, luxurious taste. Traditionally, brioche dough is a bit more challenging to handle due to its high fat content, but the result is a light, pillowy crumb once baked. The bread has a golden color and a slightly shiny crust, often thanks to an egg wash applied before baking. It is versatile, commonly used for everything from breakfast pastries to gourmet sandwiches. Brioche can be shaped into loaves, rolls, or even intricate braided forms. Its decadent nature makes it a favorite for pairing with both sweet and savory fillings, like jam, chocolate, or ham and cheese.
