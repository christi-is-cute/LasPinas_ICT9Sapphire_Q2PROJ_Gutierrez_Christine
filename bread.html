Focaccia

Focaccia is a traditional Italian bread known for its soft, airy texture and slightly crispy crust. It is made from simple ingredients such as flour, water, olive oil, salt, and yeast, with a characteristic dimpled surface that gives it its unique look and texture. The dough is typically allowed to rise for a few hours before baking, resulting in a light and fluffy interior. Focaccia can be flavored with a variety of toppings, including rosemary, garlic, olives, or sun-dried tomatoes, which infuse the bread with aromatic tastes. Often served as an appetizer, snack, or side dish, focaccia pairs wonderfully with olive oil, balsamic vinegar, or cheese.
