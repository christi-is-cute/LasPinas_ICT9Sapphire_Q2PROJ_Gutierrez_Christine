Cacio E Pepe

Cacio e pepe is a classic Roman pasta dish that celebrates simplicity and bold flavors. Its name translates to "cheese and pepper," highlighting its two main ingredients: Pecorino Romano cheese and freshly cracked black pepper. Traditionally made with pasta like spaghetti or tonnarelli, the dish relies on starchy pasta water to create a creamy, luxurious sauce without the need for cream. The pepper is toasted to enhance its aroma, adding a sharp, earthy kick that balances the salty, tangy Pecorino.
