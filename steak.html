Ribeye Steak

Sourdough is a type of bread made using a natural fermentation process involving wild yeast and lactic acid bacteria. Unlike breads made with commercial yeast, sourdough relies on a "starter," a mixture of flour and water that ferments over time to develop its distinctive tangy flavor. The long fermentation process not only enhances its taste but also makes the bread easier to digest and improves its nutritional value. Sourdough has a crusty exterior and a chewy, airy interior, making it a favorite among bread enthusiasts. It is highly versatile, perfect for sandwiches, toasts, or simply paired with butter or olive oil. This ancient bread-making technique dates back thousands of years and remains a cherished method in many cultures worldwide. Its unique taste and artisan appeal have led to a resurgence in popularity among home bakers and professional bakers alike.

Ingredients

2 (1 1/4-pound) bone-in rib eye steaks
2 1/2 teaspoons kosher salt, plus more to taste
1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper, plus more to taste
2 tablespoons canola oil
4 tablespoons unsalted butter
4 thyme sprigs
3 garlic cloves
1 rosemary sprig
